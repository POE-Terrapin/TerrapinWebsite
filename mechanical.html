Mechanical Subsystem

The final iteration of the turtle-inspired robot involved a five part system: the electronics bay, leg linkage, head, tail, and terrarium.

Electronics Bay

The guts and brain of our robot are contained in the electronics bay. The solid lower part of the shell creates an aesthetic housing for all of the electronics: servos, motors, an Arduino, Raspberry Pi, breadboards, and wires. All of the other mechanical systems attach to this main part.

Leg Linkage

The most important aspect of our turtle is its movement, and the leg linkage provides three degrees of freedom of movement to actuate the robot. A Klann linkage mimics the placing of the foot outwards and pulling it in under a turtleâ€™s body. A servo connected by a bell crank to the motor that drives the linkage moves the leg in a sweeping motion from left to right. A foot attachment with a rubbery covering ensures a good connection with the ground to allow the leg to push hard and move the turtle.

Head

The head is home to the eyes of the turtle, IR sensors, and a photoresistor. It is essentially a sensor mount, but its round shape gives the turtle its character and charm. One IR sensor is mounted as the mouth of the turtle, while the rest are supported under the head to point at opposite angles away from the straight path.

Tail

To complete the look of the turtle, the tail pokes out in the back with another photoresistor mount, It also contains a power switch for the entire robot which is easily accessible.

Terrarium

The terrarium completes the robot with a transparent dome that covers a plant. An inner ring retains the soil, preventing it from seeping outwards onto other parts of the robot. The bottom plate completes the isolation of this dirt and water system from the electronics bay. Wooden pegs in the plate provide an easy way to attach and detach the terrarium from the robot.
