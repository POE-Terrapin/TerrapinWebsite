This is Terrapin

A mobile robotic turtle with a terrarium on its back.

Our goal:

For the Olin College Principles of Engineering course, our team wanted to create a mobile turtle terrarium that would meander into and out of sunlight depending on its plants' needs and it would autonomously make its own decisions on where and when to move. Each individual in our team had different learning goals for this project, including learning more about sensors and control, modular design, communication between devices, and rapid-iterative mechanical prototyping.

Mechanical:

A bioinspired robot that moves and looks like a turtle.

Software:

Avoids obstacles and uses a decisions algorithm so the turtle can decide autonomously when and where to move.

Electrical:

Senses obstacles, surrounding light, and surrounding temperature, and it measures soil moisture content. It also powers the robot.
>

Inspiration

The inspiration came when one of our team members attended a talk about a mysterious Nemo's Garden. It turned out to be an awesome project where plants were being grown in huge biospheres underwater, offshore in Italy! Sensor data and live video from the biospheres were transmitted to shore for researchers to monitor. You can find out more about their projects here.
